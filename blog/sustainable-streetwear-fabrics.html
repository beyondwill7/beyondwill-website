10 Sustainable Fabrics Redefining Modern Streetwear

By Beyondwill LLP

Eco-conscious fashion is more than a trend — it's the new standard. Today’s streetwear brands are blending bold aesthetics with ethical responsibility. Here's a breakdown of 10 sustainable fabrics pushing the movement forward.

1. Organic Cotton

Grown without harmful chemicals, organic cotton reduces water usage and soil damage. It’s breathable, durable, and streetwear ready.

2. Hemp

Highly renewable and naturally pest-resistant, hemp is one of the most eco-friendly crops. It creates sturdy, long-lasting garments.

3. Tencel (Lyocell)

Made from wood pulp in a closed-loop process, Tencel is soft, breathable, and biodegradable — ideal for t-shirts and lining.

4. Recycled Polyester (rPET)

Created from recycled plastic bottles, rPET diverts waste from landfills and reduces carbon footprint while mimicking traditional poly aesthetics.

5. Bamboo

Fast-growing and naturally antibacterial, bamboo fabric is ultra-soft and requires minimal water or pesticides to grow.

6. Piñatex

A leather alternative made from pineapple leaves. Piñatex is cruelty-free and perfect for vegan-friendly accessories and trims.

7. Econyl

Regenerated nylon made from ocean and landfill waste. Used in activewear and fashion-forward street pieces alike.

8. Linen

Derived from flax plants, linen is biodegradable, lightweight, and has a naturally relaxed street aesthetic.

9. Cupro

A byproduct of cotton linter waste, cupro feels like silk but is plant-based. Common in high-end streetwear linings.

10. Deadstock Fabric

Repurposed leftover fabric from larger brands. Gives new life to existing materials and adds exclusive value to designs.

Why It Matters

Brands like Soulmonk incorporate sustainable fabrics into every collection — not as an afterthought but as part of the design DNA. It shows that fashion and responsibility can coexist.
